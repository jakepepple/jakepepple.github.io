So now I'm gonna start writing some text on drums. I really enjoy playing drums, teaching drums, listening to drummers, and talking about drums. It's a relaxing activity, a therapeutic activity, and a creatively fulfilling activity. It also can release stress after a long day of work, dealing with kids, coding, etc...

The modern drum kit was invented near the beginning of the 20th century, and took some modifications along the way to get to its current form. Drums had a large part in determining the course of popular music in the 20th century, and beyond. When jazz drummers starting keeping a backbeat going through songs (snare drum played on the 2nd and 4th beats) the modern popular drum groove was born, and music would never be the same.

Think about your favorite song, any song. Try listening to the drummer (or drum track). Notice how most of the drums coincide with another instrument (or another sound). This isn't a coincidence! Many drummers (or producers) use drums as the rhythmic backbone for building a song. The bass drum may coincide with the bass guitarist's line, or the hi-hat may mimic the guitar, or the snare drum may build with a synth in electronic music, etc. It all works together!
